Kmeans-and-Intrinsic-Dimensions

Posted on 2019-05-09 | In data analysis
The note for Kmeans in machine learning.

Foundation

The first thing to do is randomly locating the centroids.

Kmeans can reach the local minimum.

RMSE will always decrease or stay the same.

RMSE is the average square distance between a data point and the closest centroid.

The elbow occurs in the value of k such that the RMSE decreases rapidly below the k and decreases slowly above the k. It is the kink in the curve occurs between the steep and shallow parts of the curve. This value of k is the number of true centers.

It does not however, tell us the locations of the centers. Also recall from the previous video that finding the convergence to a global minimum is impossible with RMSE.

When the data is high-dimensional, the projection of the ring becomes harder to find. The special dimension we see the ring becomes difficult to find because it is hidden somewhere in the many dimensions. The ring becomes less, not more, easier to visualize.

High dimentions break Means. The RMSE decreases very slowly with k.

The example with more dimensions than data points did not work well because the change in RMSE was extremely tiny in that determining the elbow was nearly impossible.

In PCA, we find a hyperplane closest to the data points such that the error between any point and the plane is minimized. K-means finds a set of points such that each point has a close-by point. Also note that though it minimizes an error function, it does not attempt to find the global minimum.

Initialization

In the simple initialization, you would choose k points independently at random with uniform distribution. Because of this, the points may not represent the data well (especially if the data is not uniform). Kmeans++ is a way to start with far points without simply taking the maximal distance point. This avoids poor clustering.

We employ a data partitioning scheme in which all machines have the same representatives. Each machine partitions its own data according to its closest representative. We use the ReduceByKey to get the new means, or new representatives. Those k centers are distributed across all machines for the next iteration. This way, the same operation is done in parallel.

Data in high dimensions

Having very high dimensional data leads to concentration around value. The distance between two points in the same cluster is also the same as the distance between points of different clusters.

In high-dimensional data, the data points are very sparse. The curse of dimensionality occurs when the data is too high-dimensional and our statistical methods break down. As the dimensions get higher, the data becomes more difficult for us to visualize.

Intrinsic Dimension

Intrinsic dimension is the relationship between two dimensions, where a dimension is the number of degrees of freedom.

Epsilon represents the maximum diameter, which is the maximam distance between two points that belong in the same cell. As the number of cells increases, our epsilon decreases.

We are calculating the rate of change in the number of cells versus the diameter of the average distance. This allows us to have fractions. We are not limited to whole numbers!

Answer: 0

  1. Suppose $A$ is the line segment between 0 and 1. What is the asymptotic intrinsic dimension of $A$?

Answer: 1

  1. Suppose $A = [0,1]^5$ is the five-dimensional cube. What is the asymptotic intrinsic dimension of $A$?

Answer: 5
